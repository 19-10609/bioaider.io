LIFE TECHNOLOGY

We are developing technologies that can be used at relatively low prices and are looking for ways to effectively deliver them to both domestic and foreign countries. In the process, artificial intelligence allows people to perform tasks that they cannot do themselves. To provide or deliver goods, for example, as unmanned in areas where direct access is not available. In March 2020, the World Health Organization (WHO) launched the "Diagnosis Consortium" with its partner groups to help them receive Corona 19 medical tools and diagnostics quickly fairly in low- and medium-income countries. and The consortium was promised four months of supply by major diagnostic kit manufacturers Abbott, Sefeid, Roche and Thermofischer. According to the consortium, Sefeid's promised volume is only one-third the capacity of Corona 19 diagnostic kits. As a result, less than half of the amount requested by countries to the consortium has been achieved. Manufacturers and consortiums, including Sefeid, will reconvene to negotiate the supply and price for the next four months (September to December).
