BA provides relief items and adapts response for COVID-19 in ldilb

Deir Hassan camp in Idlib province, northwestern Syria, is one of the many camps to which hundreds of thousands of displaced families fled, to escape the military offensive by Syrian government forces with their Russian allies between December 2019 and early March 2020. Deir Hassan camp hosts more than 164,000 people in settlements scattered over the hills and, as is the case across northwest Syria, it lacks basic services. It is now also threatened by the potential spread of COVID-19. On 16 March, after assessing the needs in Deir Hassan camp, BIO AIDER (BA) distributed essential items to 180 families in the Latamneh and Al-Habeet settlements, including family tents, mats, plastic sheeting, blankets, cooking sets and hygiene kits. “We witnessed people living in the open; we also saw two or three families sharing a tent which did not protect them from the cold or the rain,” says Ahmed, BA project team leader. “There were too few tents to accommodate the new arrivals.” The next day, the BA team distributed the same relief items to 115 families in Abo Obeidah, another settlement in Deir Hassan. “Thanks to BA, we got tents,” says Manaf Shamma, a displaced mother living in Latamneh. “This camp was set up eight months ago, but it needs latrines, sewage, proper roads.”
